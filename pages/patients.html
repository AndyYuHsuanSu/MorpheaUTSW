The Basics

Morphea vs. Scleroderma — What's the difference?

These two terms get used interchangeably online — but they are not the same condition. Understanding the distinction is one of the most useful first steps.

🔵

Morphea

A localized form of scleroderma affecting only the skin and underlying tissue. It does not typically affect internal organs. Understanding your subtype matters — ask your clinician which type you have.

🟢

Systemic Scleroderma

A systemic condition that can involve skin, blood vessels, and internal organs. Early recognition and specialized care are especially important.

❓

Questions to Ask Your Doctor

What type do I have? What does monitoring look like? What symptoms should prompt me to call? Arriving prepared helps you get more from each appointment.

Appointment Guidance

Going into an appointment prepared

Appointments can feel overwhelming. These prompts help patients and families get clarity and leave with a plan.

Before you go

Write down your symptoms, when they started, and what makes them better or worse. Bring a list of all medications including supplements.

Questions to bring

"What is my specific diagnosis?" / "What should I watch for?" / "Who should I call if something changes?" / "Is there a support group you recommend?"

After your visit

Request a visit summary in writing. Note the follow-up timeline. Write down any new terms you want to research — and bring this site as a starting point.
